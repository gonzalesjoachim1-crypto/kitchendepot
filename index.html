Tomato Basil Burrata Pasta

Linda Haas

  • Preparation Time: 10 min.
  • Cooking Time: 15 min.
  • Serves: 4

A succulent but healthy meal that brings on Mexican heat with the liveliness of four herbs. Easy to prepare for quick meals or produce it when company knocks at your door.

Ingredients

  • 680 g plum tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 125 ml olive oil
  • 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 10 g chopped basil
  • 30 g grated parmesan cheese
  • 375 g burrata cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large, nonreactive bowl, combine the tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, vinegar, ½ tsp. salt and ½ tsp. pepper. Let stand at room temperature, stirring occasionally, for 1 hour.
  2. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil over high heat. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package directions until al dente. Drain the pasta well and, while still piping hot, add to the bowl with the tomato mixture.
  3. Stir in the basil and Parmesan. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  4. Divide the pasta among bowls and top each serving with 60 to 90 g of the burrata.
  5. Drizzle the bowls with olive oil and serve immediately. Serves 4 to 6.
